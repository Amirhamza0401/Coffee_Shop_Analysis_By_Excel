# Coffee_Shop_Analysis_By_Excel

## Overview

This dashboard provides a visual analysis of coffee shop sales data. It helps understand sales performance across different time periods, product categories, store locations, and product sizes. The dashboard is designed to support business decision-making by identifying trends, peak sales periods, and high-performing products.

## Key Metrics

The top section of the dashboard displays three important KPIs:

* **Total Sales:** $698,812.33 – Overall revenue generated from all transactions.
* **Total Quantity Sales:** 149,116 – Total number of products sold.
* **Average Sales:** 4.68 – Average sales value per transaction.

These indicators give a quick summary of overall business performance.

## Dashboard Components

### 1. Hourly Sales Analysis

**Chart:** Quantity ordered & Amount based on Hour
This line chart shows how sales and quantity vary throughout the day.

**Insights:**

* Peak sales occur during morning hours (around 8–10 AM).
* Sales gradually decline during later hours.
* Indicates strong morning demand for coffee products.

### 2. Monthly Sales Trend

**Chart:** Quantity ordered & Amount based on Month of Year

This chart shows the relationship between total revenue and the number of transactions across months.

**Insights:**

* Some months generate higher revenue despite similar transaction counts.
* Helps identify seasonal demand patterns.

### 3. Sales by Weekday

**Chart:** Sales Based on Week Day

Displays total quantity sold for each day of the week.

**Insights:**

* Weekdays such as Monday, Thursday, and Friday show higher activity.
* Saturday shows comparatively lower sales.

### 4. Sales by Product Category

**Chart:** Sales Based on Product (Pie Chart)

Shows the distribution of sales among different product categories.

**Major Categories:**

* Coffee
* Tea
* Bakery
* Drinking Chocolate
* Coffee Beans
* Loose Tea
* Branded Items
* Flavours
* Packaged Chocolate

**Insights:**

* Coffee contributes the largest share of total sales.
* Tea and bakery products also represent significant portions.

### 5. Sales by Store Location

**Chart:** Sales Based on Store Location (Bar Chart)

Compares sales across different store locations.

**Locations:**

* Astoria
* Hell's Kitchen
* Lower Manhattan

**Insights:**

* Hell’s Kitchen generates the highest sales.
* Lower Manhattan has the lowest sales among the three locations.

### 6. Sales by Product Size

**Chart:** Sales Based on Size (Pie Chart)

Shows the distribution of sales by product size.

**Sizes Included:**

* Large
* Regular
* Small
* Not Defined

**Insights:**

* Large and Regular sizes dominate sales.
* Small size accounts for a smaller share.

## Filters (Slicers)

The dashboard includes interactive filters to refine analysis:

* **Day Name Filter:** Allows viewing sales for specific days of the week.
* **Month Name Filter:** Allows analysis by specific months.

These filters enable dynamic exploration of the data.

## Purpose of the Dashboard

The dashboard helps stakeholders:

* Identify peak sales hours
* Understand customer purchasing behavior
* Compare performance across store locations
* Analyze popular product categories
* Support marketing and inventory planning decisions

## Tools Used

* Microsoft Excel
* Pivot Tables
* Pivot Charts
* Slicers
* Data Visualization Techniques

## Conclusion

This dashboard provides a clear and interactive overview of coffee shop sales performance. By analyzing sales patterns across time, product categories, and store locations, businesses can make informed decisions to improve operations, marketing strategies, and customer satisfaction.
